## Overall Impression

The product works, but it asks the user to manage the workflow instead of leading them through it. It currently feels like a scorer plus editor plus AI panel all sharing equal volume. The biggest opportunity is to turn the result side into a decision cockpit: one top problem, one recommended action, one safe apply/export path.

## What's Working

- The core loop is concrete: load card, analyze, see score, apply improvements, export JSON or PNG.
- AI provider settings are responsibly tucked into progressive disclosure and include a privacy warning.
- Status and error handling are better than prototype level; invalid input does not strand the user.

## Priority Issues

**[P1] The primary workflow has no dominant path**  
Why it matters: six actions sit together before the user understands the rhythm: Analyze, Load sample, Undo, Copy JSON, Download JSON, Download PNG.  
Fix: make Analyze dominant, move sample into the empty state, hide Undo until something is applied, and group exports into one post-analysis export area.  
Suggested command: `$impeccable layout`

**[P1] Results are informational, not decisional enough**  
Why it matters: the app promises evaluation and improvement, but the user still has to synthesize score, next move, findings, suggestions, essence, and AI output.  
Fix: make Next move the command center with one top issue, one recommended apply action, one preview, and one expected improvement.  
Suggested command: `$impeccable distill`

**[P2] The visual identity is too generic for a specialist craft tool**  
Why it matters: character-card creators judge nuance; the current interface feels like a generic form validator.  
Fix: keep a restrained product UI but reduce beige warmth, sharpen typography/hierarchy, and make evidence/quality language feel more like a card lab.  
Suggested command: `$impeccable colorize`

**[P2] Raw JSON editing lacks confidence scaffolding**  
Why it matters: users may fear breaking the card, especially after applying AI suggestions or exporting PNG metadata.  
Fix: add validation state, dirty-state indicator, last analyzed timestamp, before/after preview, and clear copy for PNG export availability.  
Suggested command: `$impeccable harden`

**[P2] Accessibility and efficiency need a pass**  
Why it matters: dense controls and generated cards need stronger focus states and keyboard flow; disabled button contrast is below AA at roughly 3.91:1.  
Fix: improve disabled contrast, add visible focus styles, keyboard shortcuts for Analyze/Undo, and verify details/summary focus behavior.  
Suggested command: `$impeccable audit`

## Minor Observations

- `Download PNG` being disabled is correct, but the reason should be visible without hover.
- `Run review` is vague; use a label like `Draft AI improvements` or `Run AI review`.
- The score needs a visible `/100` label.
- The top intro is slightly marketing-like for a tool; the working surface should take priority sooner.
- AI settings disclosure is a strength; keep it collapsed by default.

## Questions to Consider

- Is CardQualifier primarily a scorer, an editor, or a repair assistant?
- What would make a creator trust Apply without opening every draft first?
- Should JSON stay central, or become the advanced escape hatch after a structured card preview exists?
